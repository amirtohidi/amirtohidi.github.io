--- layout: page --- I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Computational Social Science Lab at the University of Pennsylvania, and a Knight Fellow at the Center for Media, Technology, and Democracy.

I obtained my Ph.D. in the Social and Engineering Systems program at the MIT Institute for Data, Systems, and Society, and in Statistics through the Interdisciplinary Program in Statistics. I was also affiliated with the Laboratory for Information and Decision Systems.

I received my Bachelor’s degree with a double major in Electrical Engineering and Physics from the Sharif University of Technology.

My research lies at the intersection of social science, data science, and public policy. I study information ecosystems—particularly media bias and misinformation—and how they shape public opinion and societal outcomes. I use computational and experimental methods to examine how narratives are constructed and disseminated in the digital age.
